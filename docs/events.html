Spring Art Show Opening | Wednesday, April 20, 2022: 5 - 6pm

In Person (Behrend campus): upper level of Kochel near the entrance to the Library

This exhibition inaugurates a dedicated section of physical exhibition space on the upper level of Kochel near the entrance to the Library. We also continue a tradition established last year of hosting digital works in our virtual gallery space.

Adam Holquist, Modular Synthesizer Performance | Wednesday, April 20, 2022: 7pm

In Person (Behrend campus): Bruno's.

Holquist is part of Onewayness (https://onewayness.com/), and he'll perform and host Q&A at the event.

Onewayness is Adam Holquist, a composer, improviser, and multi-instrumentalist from Erie, Pennsylvania. He uses guitars, pianos, analog and digital synthesis, spoken word and field recordings, and a variety of hardware and software tools to create atmospheric and textural music which draws influence from a variety of sources. Presented by The Mary Behrend Cultural Fund.

Sherlock Holmes on the Radio Airwaves: Schreyer Thesis Defense + Tea Dueling | Friday, April 15, 2022: 4:30 - 5:50pm.

In Person (Behrend campus): Lilley Library 12

DIGIT senior Jacqueline Chan defends her Schreyer Honors College thesis and digital project, “Sherlock Holmes on the Radio Airwaves: A Digital Remediation and Analysis” against all courteous and well-informed attacks. This is a momentous occasion as Jackie is the first DIGIT student attempting a Schreyer Honors thesis! We are serving tea and British tea biscuits, with a round of an authentic Steampunk Tea Dueling to conclude the event.

Learn about Website Generators: Ruby/Jekyll and Node.JS | Thursday, April 14, 2022: 6:30 - 8:15pm.

Join us as DIGIT senior Mia Borgia and Dr. Elisa Beshero-Bondar demonstrate what static and dynamic web development environments like Ruby/Jekyll and Node.js are all about, and how you develop websites with them. This is the first of our April 2022 series of DIGIT Works series of events and presentations.
